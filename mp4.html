Summer Quinoa Salad A light, protein-packed salad that's perfect for warm weather. Rinse & Boil: Rinse 1/2 cup of quinoa under cold water. In a small pot, bring 1 cup of water (or vegetable broth) to a boil. Add the quinoa, cover, and simmer on low for 15 minutes until water is absorbed. Fluff & Cool: Remove from heat and let it sit covered for 5 minutes. Fluff with a fork and spread it out on a plate to cool to room temperature. Chop the Veg: While the quinoa cools, dice 1/2 a cucumber, 1/4 of a red onion, and halve a handful of cherry tomatoes. The Dressing: Whisk together 2 tbsp olive oil, juice of half a lemon, 1 tsp honey, and a pinch of dried oregano. Assemble: In a large bowl, combine the cooled quinoa, chopped vegetables, and a handful of crumbled feta. Toss: Pour the dressing over the mix and toss gently. Top with fresh parsley or mint for a bright finish.

Berry Smoothie Bowl The key to a perfect "thick" bowl is using frozen fruit and very little liquid so your toppings don't sink! Freeze Your Fruit: Use 1 frozen banana (sliced before freezing) and 1 heaped cup of mixed frozen berries (strawberries, blueberries, and raspberries). The Base Liquid: Pour only 1/4 cup of milk (oat, coconut, or dairy) into your blender first. Adding the liquid first helps the blades move. Blend & Pulse: Add the frozen fruit and 1 tablespoon of Greek yogurt (optional, for creaminess). Start your blender on the lowest speed or use the "pulse" setting. Pro Tip: Use a tamper or a long spoon (with the blender off) to push the fruit down until it reaches a thick, soft-serve ice cream consistency. The Pour: Scoop the thick mixture into a wide, shallow bowl. Smooth the top with the back of a spoon. Aesthetic Toppings: Arrange these in neat rows or a crescent moon shape for a professional look: Fresh Fruit: Sliced strawberries and a few fresh blackberries. Crunch: 2 tablespoons of granola or toasted coconut flakes. Superfoods: A sprinkle of chia seeds or hemp hearts. The Drizzle: A small swirl of honey or almond butter.

Rustic Pepperoni Pizza A "rustic" pizza focuses on high heat and a hand-stretched, irregular shape. Blast the Heat: Preheat your oven to its maximum temperature (260°C / 500°F). If you have a baking stone or steel, put it in now. Stretch by Hand: On a floured surface, press a ball of pizza dough into a flat disc. Use your knuckles to gently stretch it into a rough oval. Don't use a rolling pin—keeping the air bubbles in the dough makes the crust rustic and chewy. Simple Sauce: Spread a thin layer of crushed San Marzano tomatoes over the dough. Season with a pinch of sea salt and garlic flakes. Cheesy Layers: Distribute chunks of fresh mozzarella (torn by hand) and a generous layer of pepperoni slices. The Bake: Slide the pizza onto the hot stone or a pre-heated tray. Bake for 8-10 minutes until the edges are charred and the pepperoni is crispy. Final Touch: Brush the crust lightly with garlic butter as soon as it comes out of the oven.
